### Biggest Lesson of 2025

Growing up, I dreamed of joining the Indian Air Force as an aviator. I joined NCC on 9th standard & in college which helped me develope my personality to align with that dream. My childhood was filled with activities and sports, many of which were directly or indirectly connected to that aspiration.

Once I was eligible for AFCAT/CDS exams, I underwent two SSB interviews, and failed at both. My family members, friends and mentors often wondered why I didn’t give more attempts. They believed in my potential, and even wanted me to go for UPSC.

When I graduated from college, I asked my parents for the time. I didn’t have a plan, just a desire to avoid regret. I was comfortable with failures, but not with abandoning my aspirations. They gave me two years to prove the value of the path I choose. It’s been a year, and I’ve already earned their trust and confidence, now I believe I can contribute meaningfully to something larger.

While I achieved this goal before deadline, I had to let go off my "dream" of joining the Air Force. Last one year of experimentation taught a crucial lesson.

> You don’t have to do what you want, but rather, do what’s needed.

It shifted my perspective, prioritizing community over personal needs. The butterfly effect explains how seemingly small events can have a significant impact on a journey.

My experiences have reinforced my aptitude for community-focused goals, rather than solely pursuing personal ambitions.

It’s a challenging shift, and it continues to be. I often find myself constantly dreaming about flying, Dogfighting, Academy training and then abruptly returning to reality, resuming my engineering projects ;)
