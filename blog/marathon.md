### My First Marathon Experience

You might have noticed I've been quiet for the last four months. It wasn't a random break. I needed time to get some clarity, find direction, and build momentum again.

Most of the big projects I started before October 2025 didn't work out. And when the pressure from my family added to that, things got really tough. I'm not someone who cares much about what strangers think; but when it's your own people, it hits harder.

I don't give up easily. I've always believed in pushing through, even when things get hard. That's why I turned to something I've always trusted: sports. This time, I chose running; specifically, an endurance sport that's easy to start.

I ran **42.25 km** in **4 hour 57 min** with training of just **30 days**. You can check out my detailed instagram post [here](https://www.instagram.com/p/DSnFmexCEnI/).

Here's what I learned:

- Pain is temporary, consistency gives results. The longest you can run depends on how long you can keep going through discomfort. That's true in life too. You don't win by being the fastest-you win by staying in the game long enough to get through the tough parts. It's not about talent. It's about mental strength.

- Slow, and steady wins the race. In my training, I'd often start strong-full of energy, running fast for the first 500-800 meters; then crash hard. I'd slow down for 2-3 km, and my overall pace would drop. But when I stuck to a steady, even pace, my results improved a lot. In work and life, motivation gets you started. But discipline keeps you going. You don't need to feel inspired every day. You just need to keep showing up.

This experience gave me the clarity I needed.

Here's to running the long game.
