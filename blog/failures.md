### My Failure Experiences

I am excited to share that I have successfully failed at most of the initiatives that I took in the past one year. Here is the list:

- Campus Placement
- Conference out in two SSB Interview
- Fellowship at IITB (They almost selected me and then ghosted me)
- Two Startups (One failed before even starting, One Stalled just a few days before the launch)
- My co-founder left (Because I could not manage him)
Yes, I failed at all of the above.

Consider me as a type of student who got more than what I asked for, till Graduation. I never had to face any of these failures till the age of 21 (I'm 22 now).

What I learned in the past 1 year is way more than what I could ever learn if I had taken a job or gone for higher study or any other career path.

- I stressed
- I doubted
- I cried and cried and cried....
This was my actual routine for many days.

My top learnings from these routines:

- Fail Early, Fail Fast (Failure is inevitable)
- Execute, don't just talk !
- Be utterly "shameless" to ask for help

The path ahead is:

- Tough but doable.
- Non-traditional but fulfilling.
- Uncertain but exciting.

Then what am I doing currently?

- Volunteering at three organizations
- Freelance work for my side income
- Startup Building (~Most of the time)

I'm not writing this to get your sympathy. I don't like it. Thank you, but keep it to yourself.

I have been quietly working for the past 1 year without making any noise. I build, I break, I rebuild and I repeat.

This is for anyone who is thinking to take this path and don't want to settle for "less".

If you are thinking to join this race, then "Welcome".

The only message I have for you is:

*Happy Failing !*
